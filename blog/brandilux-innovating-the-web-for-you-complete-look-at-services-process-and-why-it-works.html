Design & Development Process

Brandilux follows a predictable, client-friendly process that balances creativity with delivery discipline:

  1. Discovery & Research: Stakeholder interviews, user persona mapping, competitor analysis, and defining success metrics.
  2. Strategy & Planning: Roadmaps, prioritized features, technical architecture, and content strategy.
  3. Design: Wireframes → prototypes → design system (colors, typography, components).
  4. Development: Iterative sprints, modular components, accessible markup, CMS integration, and automated testing.
  5. QA & Launch: Cross-browser testing, performance benchmarking, accessibility checks, and deployment with rollback plans.
  6. Post-launch & Growth: Analytics review, SEO optimization, A/B testing, and scoped feature enhancements.

Common Challenges & Brandilux Solutions

  • Scope creep / unclear requirements: Clear discovery docs, phased delivery, and weekly stakeholder demos keep scope aligned.
  • Site performance & Core Web Vitals: Optimized images, lazy loading, code splitting, and CDNs ensure fast load times.
  • Security & data protection: Secure deployment pipelines, HTTPS, input validation, role-based access, and scheduled patching.

Frequently Asked Questions

How long does a typical website project take?
Small sites: 3–6 weeks. Medium sites / e-commerce: 6–12+ weeks. Timeline depends on content readiness and scope.
Do you provide hosting and maintenance?
Yes — Brandilux offers hosting guidance or fully managed hosting + maintenance plans including updates, backups, and security patches.
Which CMS or tech stack should I choose?
It depends on scale: WordPress or headless CMS for content-heavy sites, React/Next for dynamic apps, or serverless/static approaches for performance-first sites.
Can I update the site myself after launch?
Absolutely — CMS training and documentation are provided. For more complex features, ongoing support is available.
